Healthy Banana Bread

The best no-added sugar banana bread - that actually tastes good!

If you're at a point in life where you're trying to eat a little healthier, you may be cutting back on your added sugar.

However, that definitely doesn't mean you have to cut out dessert - just a few ripe bananas and (optional) chocolate chips, and you'll be able to satisfy your sweet tooth!

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams peeled, overripe bananas
  • 1/4 cup olive oil or oil of your choice
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 1/2 cups oat flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Optional: 1/2 cup 70% cacao chocolate chips. *See note

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325F and lightly grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. Mix the wet ingredients. In a large bowl, combine the bananas, oil, eggs, and vanilla extract and mix with a hand mixer or immersion blender until well combined.
  3. Add the dry ingredients. Add the flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and chcolate chips (if adding); stir just until combined. Pour batter into your prepared loaf pan.
  4. Bake and cool. Bake for 60 to 75 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out completely clean. Allow the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack and let it cool another 15-20 minutes before slicing.

Note: If adding a 1/2 cup chocolate chips, this will result in the bread having ~2 grams of added sugar per slice.
