Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies

What makes this cookie special?

If you're craving a gooey, chewy chocolately chip cookie, then this recipe is for you. It's super easy to make with just a few ingredients that you probably have in your pantry right now!

I wish I could say that this is a top secret family recipe that has been passed down for generations, but it's unfortunately not that exciting. However, even for a "modern" cookie, it's delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup salted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar, packed
  • 1 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, mix dry ingredients - flour, baking soda, baking powder and salt. Set aside.
  3. Cream together butter and sugars until combined.
  4. Beat in eggs and vanilla until light and fluffy (on high for about 2 minutes).
  5. Fold in the dry ingredients until combined.
  6. Fold in chocolate chips and mix well.
  7. Spoon out 2-3 Tablespoons of dough and roll into balls, and place on cookie sheets. Leave enough space between the dough balls.
  8. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are just starting to brown.
  9. Let cool (if you can wait that long!) and enjoy :)
